About Me

I am a PhD Candidate in Computer Science at New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT), working under the supervision of Prof. Shantanu Sharma. My research focuses on secure data processing, with particular emphasis on information-theoretic security and privacy-preserving database systems.

Prior to joining NJIT, I completed my MTech degree in Computer Science from Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology (IIIT), Delhi, India, in 2021. My work aims to develop practical solutions for secure cloud computing and cryptographic data protection.

Research Areas

Information-Theoretic Security

Developing unconditionally secure systems using secret-sharing techniques that remain secure regardless of computational capabilities of adversaries.

Secure Data Processing

Creating efficient algorithms for processing encrypted data while preventing information leakage through access patterns and volume analysis.

Cryptographic Techniques

Exploring advanced cryptographic methods including homomorphic encryption, searchable encryption, and multi-party computation protocols.

Privacy-Preserving Systems

Designing systems that protect user privacy while enabling efficient data analysis and retrieval in cloud environments.
